## Watch Like A Speaker

The first viewing can be simple: notice what holds attention. On the second viewing, look for choices such as pace, pause length, gestures, structure, and the way the speaker transitions between ideas.

## Borrow The Technique, Not The Personality

The goal is to study what works and then translate it into your own voice. Strong speakers do not all sound the same; they make clear choices that match their message.
