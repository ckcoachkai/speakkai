## Start Slow

The best tongue twister work begins at a speed that feels almost too easy. Slow practice lets the speaker hear the sound, feel the movement, and notice where the phrase gets tangled.

## Keep It Light

Use one phrase at a time. Try different voices, rhythms, or volume levels, then stop while the practice still feels like play.
